Proposal

My topic for the capstone project will be on an interactive time capsule. This project will center around dystopian narratives in which society has devolved into total corporate control, leading to harrowing levels of inequality in all aspects of life. Viewers will be able to look at and interact with various different objects sent from a dystopian future. These objects will be a snapshot into what the future might look like if society continues down the path of exploitation and prioritizing profits over the well being of the larger community.

I’m thinking about using 3D assets with Three.js to make the objects more interactive. The objects themselves will have a certain level of correlation to issues plaguing modern society (The objects could reflect the consequences of an issue taken to the extreme (ex: an insect preserved in amber or stasis technology could indicate that most if not all insect species have gone extinct, which directly correlates to the alarming decline of insect populations we see today)). Additionally, Typed.js could prove to be a really interesting and cool way of displaying text that matches the theme of the project. GSAP also provides a lot of animation capabilities that I can use to improve upon the visuals of the interface. Overall, I don’t believe the process will go very smoothly, as I don’t know what to expect when it comes to incorporating 3D objects into my work process.

My target audiences are young, educated people who may feel unease towards the progression of unchecked corporate power, social inequality, and climate change. I want to show them a potential future worse case scenario where no action is taken to improve the world and greed is allowed to run rampant and destroy all semblances of normalcy. I hope that the bleakness of the narrative portrayed could serve as a reminder and a cautionary tale of the consequences of inaction.
